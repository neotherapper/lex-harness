## When Consumer Protection Law Applies to Rental Disputes

### Brusse v Jahani BV (C-488/11) - ECJ 2013

**Ruling:** EU Unfair Terms Directive 93/13/EEC applies to residential tenancies when:
- Landlord acts "for purposes relating to trade, business or profession" (professional accommodation provider) ✓
- Tenant acts for non-commercial purposes (personal residence) ✓

**Source:** [EUR-Lex - C-488/11](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=CELEX:62011CJ0488)

**Key Quote (Paragraph 34):**
> "The directive must be interpreted as meaning that... it applies to a **residential tenancy agreement** concluded between a **landlord acting for purposes relating to his trade, business or profession** and a **tenant acting for purposes which do not relate to his trade, business or profession**."

### RWE Vertrieb AG (C-92/11) - ECJ 2013

**Ruling:** Contracts must set out in transparent fashion the reason for and method of any charge variation:
> "Consumers must be able to foresee, on the basis of clear, intelligible criteria, alterations to charges. The mere reference to legislation does not satisfy information obligations."

**Source:** [EUR-Lex - C-92/11](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=CELEX:62011CJ0092)

**Application:** A professional accommodation provider's charges without itemized third-party invoices or methodology violates this transparency standard.

### EU Directive 2006/123/EC — Services Directive

**Article 22 (Information obligations):**
> "Service providers must **demonstrate compliance** with information obligations and **demonstrate that information is accurate**."

**Article 6(9) — Burden of Proof:**
> "As regards compliance with information requirements... **the burden of proof shall be on the trader**."

**Application:** A professional service provider bears the burden of proving the accuracy and completeness of all charge documentation. If they cannot produce records, their claims fail.

## KEY PROVISIONS OF LAW 2251/1994

### Article 2 - General Transaction Terms (Γενικοί Όροι Συναλλαγών)

**Greek Summary:**
> "Γενικοί όροι των συναλλαγών που έχουν ως αποτέλεσμα την υπέρμετρη διατάραξη της ισορροπίας των δικαιωμάτων και υποχρεώσεων των συμβαλλομένων σε βάρος του καταναλωτή απαγορεύονται και είναι **άκυροι**."

**English:**
> "General transaction terms that result in excessive disturbance of the balance of rights and obligations of the parties to the detriment of the consumer are prohibited and **VOID**."

### Specific Unfair Terms (Article 2, Paragraph 7)

The following are presumptively unfair:

| Subparagraph | Greek | English | Application |
|--------------|-------|---------|-------------|
| (λ) | Υπέρμετρη οικονομική επιβάρυνση | Excessive financial burden | Significant charge claims without justification |
| (λβ) | Αποζημίωση χωρίς απόδειξη ζημίας | Compensation without proof of damage | No invoices provided |

### Article 2, Paragraph 6 - Imbalance Clause

Terms causing significant imbalance in rights/obligations to consumer's detriment = **VOID**

**Application:** Retaining a significant portion of the deposit without documentation creates significant imbalance.

### Article 2, Paragraph 10 - Burden of Proof

> "Ο προμηθευτής φέρει το βάρος της απόδειξης ότι ο συγκεκριμένος όρος αποτέλεσε αντικείμενο ατομικής διαπραγμάτευσης."

**Translation:** The supplier bears the burden of proving that a specific term was individually negotiated.

**Application:** Standard form lease contract = terms NOT individually negotiated = consumer protection applies.

## DEFINITIONS

### Consumer (Καταναλωτής)

Any natural person acting for purposes outside their trade, business, or profession.

**Application:** The tenant renting as personal residence = consumer (even if technically an LTD — purpose matters, not entity type).

### Supplier/Provider (Προμηθευτής)

Any natural or legal person acting in the exercise of their professional or business activity.

**Application:** A professional property management company = supplier.

## LAW 3587/2007 - Unfair Commercial Practices (Articles 9a-9h)

**Source:** [Lawspot.gr - Law 3587/2007](https://www.lawspot.gr/nomikes-plirofories/nomothesia/nomos-3587-2007)
**Purpose:** Transposition of EU Directive 2005/29/EC into Greek law by adding Articles 9a-9h to Law 2251/1994

### EU Directive 2005/29/EC (Parent Directive)

**Source:** [EUR-Lex - Directive 2005/29/EC](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2005/29/oj/eng)
**Consolidated version:** [EUR-Lex - Consolidated 2022](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/dir/2005/29/2022-05-28/eng)

**Key provision (Article 5):**
> A commercial practice shall be unfair if: (a) it is contrary to the requirements of **professional diligence**, and (b) it materially distorts or is likely to distort the **economic behaviour** of the average consumer.

### Application to Rental Disputes Involving a Professional Accommodation Provider

**Article 9γ - General Prohibition:**
A commercial practice is unfair if it:
1. Is contrary to **professional diligence** (επαγγελματική ευσυνειδησία) ✓
2. Materially distorts **economic behaviour** of average consumer ✓

**Article 9δ - Misleading Actions:**
A practice is misleading if it contains false information or deceives the average consumer regarding:
- The **nature** of the product/service
- The **main characteristics** of the product/service
- The **need** for replacement or repair

**Application:** Characterizing a functional item with cosmetic wear as "essentially useless" to justify full replacement cost = misleading assertion about the nature/characteristics of the item.

## LAW 4933/2022 - Aggressive Commercial Practices

Amends Law 2251/1994 to strengthen protections.

### Article 9ζ - Aggressive Practices

Defines harassment, coercion, abuse of influence as aggressive commercial practices.

**Penalties:** Up to €1.5 million

**Enforcement Examples:**
| Company | Fine | Date |
|---------|------|------|
| doValue | €435,000 | December 2023 |
| Cepal Hellas | €235,000 | December 2023 |

**Application:** Persistent demands for disputed charges without documentation, causing distress = potentially aggressive practice.

### Full Penalty Table (Law 2251/1994 as amended)

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Base administrative fine | €1,500 - €1,000,000 |
| Repeat offenders | Fine may be **doubled** |
| Continued non-compliance | Up to **1% of global turnover** |
| Serious cases | Suspension of operations up to 1 year |

### Consumer Remedies (Law 4933/2022)

Since May 20, 2022 (Law publication date), consumers harmed by unfair practices can seek:
- **Compensation for damages**
- **Price reduction**
- **Contract termination**
- **Repair or replacement**

## LAW 5255/2025 — New Consumer Protection Authority

**Source:** [Taxheaven.gr - Law 5255/2025](https://www.taxheaven.gr/law/5255/2025)

Establishes the new independent Consumer Protection Authority, replacing the General Secretariat for Consumer Protection. Strengthens enforcement capacity for unfair commercial practices.

## EU DIRECTIVE 93/13/EEC

**Source:** [EUR-Lex - Directive 93/13/EEC](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:31993L0013)

### Annex - Indicative List of Unfair Terms

**Point 1(d):** Terms permitting the seller/supplier to retain sums paid by consumer where consumer decides not to conclude or perform the contract, without providing for consumer to receive equivalent compensation.

**Point 1(e):** Terms requiring consumer who fails to fulfill obligation to pay disproportionately high sum in compensation.

**Application:** Retention of a significant portion of a deposit without proper documentation is potentially unfair under this directive.
